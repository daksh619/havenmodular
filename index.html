Planning & Compliance

We check every site.
Before we quote.

Havenmodular is a compliance-led business. We don't make blanket promises. We make honest assessments.

"Designed for the 32–45 sqm auxiliary dwelling market, subject to final regulations, site suitability, and compliance review. Planning and compliance route checked per site before final quotation. Building Regulations, services, access, and site conditions must be reviewed before installation."

What this means for you.

Ireland's planning landscape for auxiliary dwellings is evolving. We monitor regulatory changes and apply them carefully to each site. Here's what we check before committing to any quote:

🏛️
Planning Route Assessment
We assess whether your site requires planning permission or falls within exempted development thresholds. Every site is different. County, zone, and existing structures all matter. We never make a blanket promise.
📐
Building Regulations
Planning permission and Building Regulations are separate. All Havenmodular homes must comply with Irish Building Regulations (fire safety, insulation, structural integrity, ventilation) regardless of planning route.
🚗
Site Access & Delivery
Crane access, roadway width, overhead cables and gate width are all reviewed in advance. If access is restricted, we identify solutions before contracts are signed.
💧
Wastewater & Drainage
Connection to existing wastewater system must be reviewed. In some cases, an independent wastewater treatment system may be required. We assess this per site.
⚡
Utility Connections
Water, electricity, and broadband must be extended to the new structure. We review proximity, cost implications, and installation routes as part of the site check.
🌿
BER & Energy Performance
Every Havenmodular home is designed to achieve A1–A2 BER. BER assessment is included in our compliance support packages and is required for any habitable dwelling.
🧱
Foundations & Ground Conditions
Ground conditions vary significantly across Ireland. A standard foundation allowance is included, but abnormal conditions such as rock, poor drainage, or fill material will affect the final quote.
🔥
Fire Safety
Separation distances from boundary and existing structures, fire-rated construction, and escape routes are all assessed as part of our pre-quote compliance review.

Construction System

Built for modern
Irish garden living.

Premium modular construction, carefully selected materials, and a practical build system for comfort, strength and everyday use.

At Havenmodular, our modular garden homes are made to feel like a proper home, not a temporary cabin. Every element of the structure is chosen to create a warm, durable and efficient living space for families who need extra room, independent accommodation, or a compact garden home.

Our construction system uses a light-gauge steel frame, insulated wall build-up, durable external boards, weather-resistant roofing layers, double-glazed windows, internal wall linings, bathroom finishes and practical flooring systems. The result is a compact home that is fast to assemble, clean in design, and suitable for modern residential use.

Smart structure. Warm interiors. Built for everyday living.

Havenmodular 45 sqm modular garden home, built for compact, independent living in Irish homes.

01
Structural Frame

Strong light-gauge
steel frame

The core structure of the home is built around a precision-engineered light-gauge steel frame. This type of frame is commonly used in modern modular and offsite construction because it is strong, straight, stable and suitable for fast assembly.

The wall frame and roof truss system uses AS1397 G550 AZ150 0.95 mm steel. It also includes structural accessories such as screws and bearers as part of the main steel structure package.

Why this matters

A steel frame gives the home a strong structural base while keeping the building system lightweight and efficient. It helps create consistent wall lines, accurate factory production, and faster on-site assembly compared with traditional block construction.

  • Light-gauge steel frame construction
  • Precision-manufactured offsite
  • Strong, stable and efficient structure
  • Made for fast assembly
  • Suitable for compact modular homes
02
External Wall System

Durable external
wall build-up

The external wall system is designed to protect the home from weather while supporting insulation and internal comfort. The specification includes 75 mm ALC external panels, wall battens, vapour barrier and 100 mm R2.5 glass wool insulation.

This creates a layered wall system rather than a simple cabin wall. Each layer has a role: external protection, cavity/support, moisture control, insulation and internal finish.

Why this matters

For a small home, wall performance is very important. The wall system needs to help retain heat, reduce cold spots, manage moisture and create a comfortable indoor environment throughout the year.

  • External ALC panel wall layer
  • Vapour barrier layer
  • Insulated wall build-up
  • Internal wall lining system
  • Built for comfort and durability
03
Roof System

Weather-resistant
roof construction

The roof system is built using steel battens, vapour barrier, reinforced board layers and waterproofing. The specification lists 35 mm steel battens, a vapour barrier, 15 mm CFC sheet reinforcement, 1.5 mm asphalt waterproofing layer, fascia, soffit, gutter and downpipe components.

Why this matters

The roof is one of the most important parts of a modular home in Ireland. A good roof system helps protect against rain, moisture and long-term weather exposure, so each element is carefully specified.

  • Reinforced roof board layer
  • Waterproofing membrane/layer
  • Steel fascia board
  • Soffit detailing
  • Aluminium gutter and downpipe system
  • Built for reliable water management
04
Insulation & Comfort

Built for warmth
and everyday comfort

The wall and ceiling systems include 100 mm R2.5 glass wool insulation. This helps improve thermal comfort and supports a warmer indoor environment.

A compact home must feel comfortable all year round. Our insulated wall and ceiling build-up is designed to help retain warmth, reduce heat loss and create a more comfortable living space. Before any Havenmodular model is offered for customer installation, the build-up will be reviewed against the relevant Irish requirements for energy performance, ventilation and moisture control.

  • Insulated wall and ceiling system
  • Supports year-round comfort
  • Helps reduce heat loss
  • Supports a warmer living environment
  • Final specification reviewed for Irish performance requirements
05
Interior Walls & Ceilings

Clean internal finish

The internal wall system includes normal gypsum board for dry areas, CFC board for wet areas, ceramic wall tiles in the kitchen splashback and bathroom areas, and skirting for dry areas. The ceiling includes normal gypsum board in dry areas and water-resistant gypsum board in wet areas. This gives the home a proper residential interior finish, with clean modern living spaces rather than exposed panels.

  • Gypsum board internal walls
  • Water-resistant board in wet areas
  • Tiled bathroom and kitchen splashback areas
  • Clean modern internal finish
  • Feels like a real home, not a temporary cabin
06
Flooring

Durable floor build-up

The specification includes 18 mm high-density CFC sheet beneath dry and wet floor areas, with 600 × 600 mm ceramic floor tiles for bathroom, laundry and other ground-floor areas.

The floor system is designed for practical everyday living. Durable board layers and tiled surfaces help create an easy-to-maintain finish suitable for kitchens, bathrooms, utility areas and general living spaces.

  • High-density floor board layer
  • Ceramic tile floor finish
  • Practical and easy to maintain
  • Suitable for wet and dry areas
  • Strong base for compact residential use
07
Windows & Doors

Double-glazed windows
and modern doors

The specification includes CE aluminium double-glass sliding windows, a steel security external door, aluminium double-glass sliding door and MDF internal doors.

Large windows and sliding doors help make a compact home feel bright, open and connected to the garden. Our design focuses on natural light, indoor-outdoor flow and a modern residential feel.

  • Double-glazed aluminium windows
  • Large sliding glass doors
  • Bright open-plan living feel

08
Bathroom & Fittings

  • Walk-in shower layout

Kitchen & Living Area

Open-plan living,
dining and kitchen

Exterior Wall Panel

The outside layer,
built for Irish weather.

Our exterior wall build-up provides structural stability, thermal performance, weather protection and a clean modern finish. Each layer has a specific role. Together, they form a wall system that far exceeds a basic cabin shell.

Layer by layer
01
Exterior Slatted Cladding
Premium wood-plastic composite slatted cladding. Modern, durable, low-maintenance finish. Available in 15 colour options including Oak, Anthracite, Walnut and Stone.
02
Rockwool / Mineral Wool Insulation
200 mm high-density mineral wool slab in the middle of the wall structure. Excellent thermal performance, fire resistance and sound absorption.
03
XPS Board
High-performance extruded polystyrene board for excellent thermal insulation and moisture resistance beneath the cladding layer.
04
Moisture Barrier Paper
Protects the structural core from moisture ingress, preventing damp and improving long-term durability.

Oriented strand board adds structural strength and rigidity to the panel. Provides a solid substrate for the moisture barrier and insulation layers.

Interior Wall Panel

The inside layer,
warm, clean and residential.

Our interior wall panel system combines structural support, insulation and a clean plasterboard finish to create a warm and comfortable internal environment. This is what separates a real home from a basic garden cabin.

Layer by layer
01
12 mm Gypsum Board (Plasterboard)
Smooth and durable internal finish. Ready for paint or wallpaper, giving the home a clean, modern, residential feel. Water-resistant board used in wet areas.
02
11 mm OSB Board
Oriented strand board adds structural rigidity and provides a strong substrate between the insulation and internal finish layers.
03
200 mm Rockwool Insulation
High-density rockwool slab in the core. Excellent thermal performance, fire resistance and sound absorption make each room genuinely comfortable.
04
90 mm LGS Frame
The structural backbone. Light-gauge steel frame that gives the entire wall its strength and precision. Non-combustible. Manufactured to ±1mm tolerance.
